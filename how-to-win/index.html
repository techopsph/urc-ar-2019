Strategy Updates

Last year, I articulated our How To Win strategies which will drive our value creation in the coming years. We are pleased that our transformation initiatives continue to manifest in our results. A more responsive supply chain, stronger partnerships with distributors and retailers, and product portfolio improvements drive robust growth across our different categories. The legacy of Mr. John drives us to ensure that we preserve and cultivate what was built by our founder and his contribution to society through our How To Win strategic areas.

Let me now discuss each of our How To Win strategies along with our progress in 2019.

I. Product Supply Chain
Transformation

Throughout the years, URC has been committed to upholding customer satisfaction and confidence. As we continue to operate our business, we aim to promote best-in-class supply chain practice, responsible production, and consumer well-being through food safety. This ongoing transformation of our operating model is key to enable better customer satisfaction, market reach, productivity, cost efficiencies, and service responsiveness in our end-to-end product supply chain. We started transforming our structure in 2018 into an integrated supply chain ensuring seamless integration from order planning, procurement, manufacturing, and logistics. Our transformation is a multi-phased journey where we pilot everything in our home market then eventually replicate it in other markets where we operate. For this pillar we have three transformational programs that will support our growth:

a. LEAN Manufacturing Excellence

We continue to adopt best practices to eliminate waste and optimize conversion and logistics costs. This transformation will entail leveraging our scale better in sales and operations planning, lean

manufacturing in one of our biggest and most diverse (in terms of manufacturing lines) facilities in the Philippines. Through this pilot we aim to create a replicable process in reducing material waste,
optimizing energy and water usage, and optimizing our manning. We expect a total of Php 1 billion in savings from LEAN over the next three years.

LEAN Manufacturing
  • Successful pilot of Lean in Calamba
  • Application of LEAN Mindset in the Philippines
  • Philippines Reduced production wastage by 30 bps
  • Water & Energy Usage Ratio improved more than 10%
Our Progress:

We are pleased to announce that we achieved our 2019 objectives of Php 200 million savings from the implementation of pilot LEAN manufacturing projects in Calamba plus the inculcation of the “LEAN mindset” across the rest of the facilities in the Philippines.

The use of the LEAN mindset enabled savings in Calamba and the other plants. Some examples would be improvements in utilities usage in both water and energy, targeted material savings, and machine efficiencies.
The LEAN mindset enabled the organization to be more responsive towards addressing issues raised by operations, who are now enabled by new capabilities via training and availability of analytical tools. The new integrated supply chain structure resulted in a more collaborative process to prevent wastage in inputting material and finished goods. As a result, we were able to reduce our wastage by 30 basis points driven by a more accurate planning of our input material versus production demand as well as processes in product handling and storage. For 2020 we expect more savings as we roll out LEAN and replicate all our learnings from the pilot to all facilities in the Philippines.

b. Better Sales & Operations Planning

Prior to the establishment of our integrated supply chain structure, we’ve experienced discrepancies in terms of forecast from different legs in the supply chain. This resulted in inaccuracies in customer order fulfillment that affected our enterprise order fill rate.

Our Progress:

In 2019 we transformed our governance to ensure that the forecast from all proponents in our value chain are aligned. As a result of this, the new governance

significantly improved the timely fulfillment of customer orders and freshness of our products. Our enterprise order fill rate reached as high as 90%+ as a result of a more robust sales and operations planning and an integrated supply chain structure.

c. Supply Network Design (SND)

SND aims to optimize our entire production network to eliminate complexity of operations driven by the design of our current network. In 2019, we had a total of 16 facilities in the Philippines for just Branded Foods Business (BCF-PH) alone. This created complexities due to the fragmented design and distribution of our lines which affects our cost to serve, produce, and utilization where scale benefits have been pulled by smaller facilities making fixed manufacturing expenses significantly more expensive than large facilities. The idea is to simplify our network into one with fewer, larger, multi-category plants which will result in higher savings and improved responsiveness. Each plant primarily serves its own region; manufacturing closer to demand is the best strategy. A simpler network will unlock value across the board. Similar to LEAN, this transformation is also a multi-phased approach and will cover both our local and international operations.

Our Progress:

Last year, we completed our Supply Network Design study and identified opportunities to improve long-term cost efficiencies while supporting future business expansion. This entailed facility rationalization, asset write-offs, and redundancies in the Philippines and Australia as we further consolidate our facilities into what we call mega-sites in the near term. As we transform our process and capabilities towards a regional approach, the end-state of our supply network design will also enable us to execute our Regional Sourcing Initiative and scale further our Global Exports business while at the same time, make us more responsive to the growing needs of our customers.

II. Partner of Choice

Our decades of experience has enabled us to gain capabilities in serving the evolving retail landscape. Our customer engagement, along with our wide product offerings and price points, made us one of the biggest suppliers of top retailers today. With the continuous development and upgrade of our distribution capabilities, we were able to reach consumers across different types of retail outlets through our account servicing in modern trade channels and exclusive distributors in traditional trade. As we grow the business, we continue to reinvest in capabilities that will drive scale and efficiency in our distribution and account management. We aim to be a strong partner of choice today and the coming years by closely collaborating with our trade customers to accommodate their needs and enhance our trade execution.

I am pleased to announce our strong progress in the following key initiatives of this strategy:

a.) General Trade: Route-To-Market Strategy

Our route-to-market strategy focuses on increasing our direct distribution (coverage), better productivity

and sales quality (lines sold) driven by consistent perfect store presence like excellent merchandising and quality of product lineup in stores. Our roadmap is to complete the penetration of 300,000 direct accounts in the Philippines from 2019 to 2021 that will be served by our exclusive national distributors. These accounts are mainly composed of small grocery chains, sari-sari-stores (SSS), and other grassroots outlets.

Our Progress:

We are pleased to announce that we had an excellent year in expanding our direct distribution coverage as we have already achieved our full-year target during the 1H of the year. With our current momentum, I am confident that we can achieve 300,000 account coverage by 2021.

DRIVING NEW CAPABILITIES

Driving Renewable Sources

We installed solar panels in one of our biggest facilities in the Philippines, a one-megawatt solar power rooftop plant in URC’s Canlubang factory in Laguna. The solar panels will cover an area of 10,000 sq.m. on the rooftop of the main production building, and will be operational from 7 a.m. to 5 p.m. Upon completion, the panels are designed to produce an average of 4,000 kilowatt hours (kWh) of clean electrical energy every 10 hours.

WATER |

We utilize water at every stage of our value creation process. We understand the importance of efficient water management, given that our water use affects operational cost, the stability of water supply, compliance with government standards, and overall business continuity.

Our commitment to sustainability means that we practice water source protection and conservation. As a company, we’ve committed to substantially improve water use in our facilities by improving our Water Use Ratio by 30%, by 2030. We are monitoring our performance based on the requirements of the National Water Resources Board (NWRB). We withdraw water from different sources and adhere to the maximum allowable extraction rate.

Driving the LEAN Mindset to conserve water

Our practices in Lean Manufacturing is a major driver in reducing water usage per unit produced. BCFG, for one, conducted a source vulnerability assessment to identify water sources at risk of becoming scarce and polluted, critical areas for deep well water use, and possible losses in lakes. 

We also recognized the importance of looking for alternative sources of water aside from the deep wells that our plants are currently using.

Upholding the discipline and practice of 3Rs

As a company we recognize that water is a non-renewable resource, and it is an essential input material in the production of our products. In URC, we practice the principle of the three Rs: Reduce, Reuse, and Recycle. Implementation of the three Rs is based on the need or requirement of the plant. An assessment is being done by comparing the actual versus the general water usage of each product category or plant. We set our priorities based on product categories or plants with high water usage.

SAFETY | Establishing our new culture of safety

At URC, we value the health and safety of our employees. We proactively instill measures in order to mitigate the risk and ensure safety and a healthy environment within the workplace. Across the business unit in all our plants and operations we implement an Environment, Health and Safety Policy that reinforces the proactive participation and awareness of all employees in owning workplace health and safety practices. We ensure that all workers and their activities within the premises of the manufacturing facility are properly onboarded.

Our commitment to safety focuses on ensuring zero lost time injuries in the workplace across the region. We measure safety by looking at Lost Time Injury Frequency Rate (LTIFR) and All Injury Frequency Rate (AIFR). For 2019, we are happy that we decreased our LTIFR from 1.18 in 2018 to 1.16 in 2019. The AIFR, on the other hand, declined from 10.05 in 2018 to 7.41 as a result of the improvement in our international operations. For BCFG, total safety incidents decreased as we increased the safety implementation programs across the operation (ex. HAND program, SLIPPAGE program).

PORTFOLIO | Building and nurturing our Wellness Criteria

Nutrition is part of our product materiality. Our 2030 commitment states that we will improve the nutrition and wellness profile of our product portfolio aligned to the URC wellness criteria. The areas for renovation are reduction of sodium, sugar, and substitution of saturated fats.

Our progress in 2019 showed that URC is deliberately pushing this area of our portfolio and we are pleased that in our snack foods, 85% of our SKUs in BCF Philippines (which covers 150 SKUs) passed one or more of the URC wellness criteria. While in beverage, 86% or 72 SKUs passed two or more of the wellness criteria. We made sure that the renovation we did will not alter or affect the quality and experience of our products that consumers continue to love. 

As a food and beverage manufacturer, we believe that consumers are changing fast due to emerging trends brought by the growing middle-class population in the region. Soft drinks were banned during the mid-2000s which is why C2 thrived when it was introduced as an alternative to consumers given that they have already started to shift towards better-for-you choices.

“ Our success factor is to ensure that we make 70% of our portfolio in the Philippines meet the URC wellness criteria as we renovate our existing products and launch new products in wellness. ”

As you can see in 2018, the government previously imposed an excise tax on sweetened beverages while some schools started to ban products that do not pass the parameters in terms of sodium, sugar, and fat set by the departments of health and education. Our investors also see this as a critical area in sustainability for URC to remain relevant in the coming years as consumer preferences change. Looking at the market, growth in wellness-related categories like dairy, packaged water, etc. has swiftly increased in the last two years.

Aside from renovations of our existing products, we also pushed the launches of new products in line with wellness like the following:

In Oceania, we are pushing more products to address the growing trends in healthier snacking in developed markets through NAFNAC (no artificial flavors, no artificial colors) as we launched products like Natural Chip Co. and Veggie Chips in Australia.

PACKAGING | Making more eco-friendly packaging

Packaging is part of our product materiality, and our 2030 commitment states that “we will reduce our packaging footprint & make 60% of our packaging recyclable.” To achieve our commitment, we are looking into the following:

1. Reduction of packaging footprint 

We look for opportunities in renovating our packaging materials especially in snack foods. We are looking into using a mono-material layer in packaging while optimizing schemes for packaging and logistics efficiency.

2. Increase the proportion of our packaging materials that are recyclable 

As technologies become available and feasible, we continue to look for opportunities in making our packaging as recyclable as possible.

In 2019 we are pleased that we were able to increase the proportion of our recyclable packaging from 60% to 80%. This has been driven by our focus towards using packaging that is currently recyclable in terms of available technologies especially in emerging markets like PET bottles, PP Cups, and paper-type packaging.

Our challenge as a company is to find ways to make films and laminates recyclable given that 60% are still difficult to recycle given they’re multi-layer properties. The good news is that 40% of our films and laminates packaging are considered recyclable since they are single-material type. To address the remaining 60%, we have to find opportunities and solutions to change their composition into a single-material type without compromising the safety, integrity, and quality of our products. 

Our 2030 commitment states that “we will reduce our packaging footprint & make 60% of our packaging recyclable.”
